01 — Forecasting
Rural Demand Proxy Engine
Cold start inventory planning for new fulfillment locations
↗ Live App GitHub 📓 Notebook

New locations have no sales history, but planners still need opening inventory decisions. Without a demand signal, teams either over-stock — tying up working capital — or under-stock, missing early demand and damaging new market relationships.

Why It Matters

Opening inventory decisions at low-history sites directly affect SKU availability, safety stock costs, and the speed at which a new location reaches operational maturity. Getting day-one wrong is expensive and hard to recover from.

Who This Is For

Inventory planners, demand planners, and supply chain analysts responsible for new site launches — especially in markets where sales data is sparse, seasonal, or unavailable.

What I Built

A proxy-based demand initialization engine that identifies the closest historical analog (sister store) for a new location using demographic, geographic, and operational similarity scores, then applies safety stock simulation to generate opening inventory recommendations with quantified uncertainty bands.

How It Works

The tool ingests location attributes, scores similarity across a configurable set of features, selects the best-fit analog store, scales demand patterns to the new location, and runs a Monte Carlo safety stock simulation to surface a recommended stocking range with confidence intervals.

Business Recommendation
Use proxy-based demand initialization plus safety stock simulation before opening low-history sites. The analog matching approach materially outperforms blank-slate planning and reduces the cost of opening inventory error.
Limitations
Proxy quality depends on the richness of the analog store database — sparse networks reduce match quality.
Safety stock simulation assumes demand follows modeled distributions; highly seasonal or lumpy demand requires additional tuning.
Does not account for local promotional calendars or macro demand shocks at launch.

02 — Risk Scoring
Intermodal Lane Risk Agent
Pre-booking freight risk scoring across major US corridors
↗ Live App GitHub

A lane can look cheap and still carry high disruption risk before freight is tendered. Standard rate comparison tools show cost, not risk — leaving freight teams exposed to delays, re-routing costs, and service failures that don't appear until after booking.

Why It Matters

Pre-booking risk scoring changes the freight decision from price-only to price-plus-risk. A lane that costs 5% more but carries 40% less disruption exposure is often the better choice — but only if you can quantify the tradeoff before tendering.

Who This Is For

Freight analysts, transportation managers, and logistics operations teams who book intermodal moves across major US corridors and want to surface risk before committing to a carrier and lane.

What I Built

A real-time lane risk scoring agent that monitors 15 major US intermodal corridors, ingests live disruption signals, scores each lane on a composite risk index, and surfaces alternate routes when risk exceeds threshold.

How It Works

The agent queries live data feeds for each corridor, applies a weighted risk model across disruption categories, produces a normalized risk score per lane with a plain-English flag reason, and ranks corridors from lowest to highest risk exposure for the current booking window.

Business Recommendation
Use lane risk scoring before booking to avoid choosing the cheapest option when disruption exposure is materially higher. A 5-minute pre-booking check against live corridor risk can prevent service failures that take weeks to recover from.
Limitations
Risk scores reflect conditions at query time — dynamic events can change rapidly after booking.
Alternate route suggestions are directional, not fully optimized for cost-risk tradeoff.
Coverage limited to 15 corridors in the current build.

03 — Compliance Triage
De Minimis Compliance Defender
Low-value import compliance triage for trade operations teams
↗ Live App GitHub
Business Problem
The Triage Problem

Low-value shipments require faster SKU-level review when policy rules, origin exposure, and classification uncertainty increase compliance workload. The May 2025 executive action suspending de minimis treatment for all PRC-origin shipments turned a once-simple clearance process into a line-by-line review problem.

Why It Matters

Without triage, compliance analysts review every SKU at the same depth regardless of actual risk. High-risk items get buried alongside items that could clear immediately. Manual review doesn't scale as manifest volume grows.

Who This Is For

Trade compliance analysts, customs brokers, and import operations teams managing low-value shipment manifests with mixed origins — particularly teams dealing with increased PRC-origin scrutiny post-May 2025.

What I Built

A live AI-powered compliance triage tool. Analysts upload a manifest CSV; the tool evaluates each SKU against a multi-factor rule engine powered by Claude Haiku and the USITC HTS Schedule, then returns a three-tier ranked review queue with flag reasons, HTS classification candidates, and duty exposure estimates.

How It Works

The rule engine checks each SKU against four risk vectors: origin country, declared value, description quality, and supplier identity. Claude Haiku provides candidate HTS chapter and heading with a confidence label. Results are sorted by risk score into an actionable review queue.

Business Recommendation
Use a ranked review queue to separate broker escalation, analyst review, and clearable SKUs before customs workflow slows down. In the demo run, 44% of SKUs cleared without analyst action — that's the efficiency gain triage delivers.
Limitations
AI-suggested classification only — not a licensed customs ruling. All HTS suggestions must be validated before clearance.
Duty rates are illustrative. Section 301 rates change frequently; verify against the official USITC schedule.
Policy snapshot reflects August 2025 guidance. Trade rules change — verify all determinations before clearance.
